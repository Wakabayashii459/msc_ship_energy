# GCC / Geopolitical Risk Extension

## Why this matters
Cruise operations in GCC and nearby regions may face:
- higher ambient temperatures
- heavier HVAC and cooling loads
- itinerary disruption risk from geopolitical tension
- port congestion or rerouting
- higher fuel and operational cost uncertainty

## How it connects to this project
This ship energy analysis can be extended by linking telemetry-based auxiliary-load behaviour to:
- route geography
- climate zone
- port stay duration
- disrupted voyage profiles
- heat-driven hotel-load increases

## Potential features for future model
- region / route label
- ambient temperature
- humidity
- port vs sea time share
- schedule deviation
- voyage disruption flag

## Business use
- estimate added HVAC demand in hot climates
- quantify operational energy penalty under route changes
- support fuel planning and generator scheduling
- improve resilience planning for itinerary disruptions

## Example presentation message
In hotter GCC deployments, auxiliary demand may increase materially because HVAC and cooling systems become more active. Under disrupted voyage patterns or port delays, hotel loads can remain elevated while propulsion drops, which changes generator loading and fuel efficiency.
